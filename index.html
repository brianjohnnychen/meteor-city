01 — Approach

Every product begins as a measurement.

Most consumer brands start with an idea and hope the market agrees. We work in the opposite direction. Skyros continuously observes what sells, what stalls, how crowded a category is, how competitors advertise, and which search terms carry real demand. When the numbers describe a gap worth filling, the product organization takes over — from industrial design through manufacturing, freight, and retail.

S1

Collect

Continuous capture of marketplace data — sales rank movement, pricing, reviews, ad placements, and search behavior.

S2

Analyze

Aggregated signals are modeled into a clear picture: what sells, what doesn't, and how defensible each category is.

S3

Design

Validated opportunities become product specifications — engineered against the exact shortcomings the data exposed.

S4

Manufacture

Production runs with vetted manufacturing partners, with compliance and quality control handled in-house.

S5

Distribute

Finished goods are freight-forwarded into North America and sold through national retail and wholesale channels.

02 — Platform

Skyros reads the market so we don't have to guess.

Skyros is the instrument every Meteor City decision runs through. It collects and aggregates marketplace data at scale, then resolves it into the signals that decide whether a product should exist. It’s an internal tool — used for our own products, and in consulting work for manufacturers.

Signal / Demand

Sales performance

Which products move and which don't — tracked over time, not sampled once.

Signal / Field

Competitive density

How crowded a category is, who holds it, and where the incumbents are weak.

Signal / Spend

Advertising behavior

What competitors pay to be seen, and what that reveals about their margins and intent.

Signal / Language

Keyword landscape

The search terms customers actually use — the demand map a category is built on.

03 — Product line

Elestoria: workspace hardware, proven by the numbers first.

Elestoria is Meteor City's computer and workspace accessory line — the physical output of the Skyros pipeline. Workspace hardware spanning single-device stands to multi-device systems, where each product exists because the data said it should.

The line serves both consumers and organizations: retail through Amazon, Walmart, and Target, and wholesale programs supplying institutional buyers — among them the United States Air Force, Amazon Corporate, and Crown-Link Corporation.
