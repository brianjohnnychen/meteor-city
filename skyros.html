Skyros.
The market, measured.

Skyros is our data collection, aggregation, and analysis platform. It watches consumer marketplaces the way a seismograph watches the ground — continuously, quantitatively, and without opinion. Everything Meteor City manufactures was first a pattern in this data.

01 — Architecture

From raw marketplace noise to a manufacturing decision.

The platform runs as a pipeline of five systems. Data enters as unstructured marketplace observations and leaves as a single, defensible answer: build, or don’t.

fig. 03 the Skyros pipeline

02 — Collection

How the data is gathered.

C-01

Observe the shelf

Skyros monitors public marketplace listings across the categories we operate in: prices, sales-rank movement, inventory signals, ratings, and review flow. Observation is continuous — a listing is a time series, not a snapshot.

C-02

Read the advertising layer

Sponsored placements reveal what listings alone can’t: which products a competitor is willing to pay to defend, how aggressively, and against which search terms. Skyros records placement patterns as a proxy for competitor margin and intent.

C-03

Map the language of demand

Search-term data shows how customers actually describe what they want — which is rarely how manufacturers describe it. Skyros maintains keyword landscapes per category: volume, seasonality, and the gap between what’s searched and what’s sold.

C-04

Resolve everything to one record

The same physical product appears under different names, bundles, and sellers across channels. An entity-resolution layer collapses these into a single canonical product record, so every downstream number describes the product — not the listing.

C-05

Keep the history

Everything lands in a longitudinal warehouse. Category questions are answered against years of history: whether demand is structural or seasonal, whether an incumbent is strengthening or coasting, and how new entrants have fared before.

04 — Consulting

For our products — and sometimes for others’.

Skyros is our internal tool — every Elestoria product decision runs through it.

Alongside our own line, we do consulting work for manufacturers. We combine their factory data with Skyros’s market data to show which products are worth launching, which face too little demand to bother, and how a launch should differ across markets. In some cases we handle the launch and distribution ourselves.
