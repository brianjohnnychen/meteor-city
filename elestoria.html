Meteor City · Product line

Elestoria.
Hardware the data asked for.

Elestoria is the physical output of the Skyros pipeline — a line of computer and workspace accessories built where the numbers showed a gap. Laptop stands, USB hubs, and docking stations, sold at retail through Amazon, Walmart, and Target, and at wholesale to institutional buyers.

01 — Design process

From CAD file to production part.

Every Elestoria product moves through the same three states. It begins as parametric CAD, engineered against the specific shortcomings Skyros found in the incumbent products. The assembly is resolved down to every spring and fastener, prototyped and lived with on real desks, and only then released to production tooling. The renders below are from the dock’s original 2022 CAD files.

Phase 1 · Drawing

Engineered to the data

Arm angle, grip travel, and the gravity-lock geometry are set by the device range the data shows people actually dock — up to 17.3″, 1.5″ thick.

Phase 2 · Exploded

Every part, accounted for

The assembly opened up: grip arms with rubber caps, the sliding rail carriage, springs, pins, and fasteners. The gravity lock is purely mechanical — nothing to power, nothing to pair.

Phase 3 · Production

The finished form

Anodized aluminum arms over the black rail carriage — CNC-formed, QC-inspected at the factory and again at the freight forwarder.

02 — Catalog

Three families. One standard.

The catalog is deliberately narrow. Elestoria enters a category only when Skyros shows a durable gap, so each family carries a small number of models that each earn their place. Renders shown are representative; retail availability varies by channel.

Laptop stands

3 models

ELE-LS01

Apex Riser

Fixed 11° single-piece aluminum riser. Open rear for airflow and cable pass-through; rated to 17″ machines.

ELE-LS02

Fold One

Scissor-fold travel stand, 6 height positions. Collapses flat to 15 mm and weighs under 300 g.

ELE-VD01

Vertical Dock

Gravity-locking vertical dock — the device’s own weight closes the grip; lift to release. Adjustable to 1.5″ thick, devices to 17.3″.

USB hubs

2 models

ELE-HB01

Travel Hub

Pocket unibody hub on an attached cable — display output, USB-A data, card readers, and pass-through charging.

ELE-HB02

Desk Hub

Full-width hub for fixed setups — dual display output, wired networking, a bank of USB ports, and host charging.

ELE-HB—

Next in family

A third hub is in the Skyros validation queue. It ships when the data says so — not before.

Docking stations

2 models

Thunderbolt Dock

Desktop dock in a steel chassis — dual display output, host charging over one cable, front-facing data and audio.

ELE-DS02

Tower Dock

Universal USB-C dock standing on a weighted base — a full port array in a footprint smaller than a coaster.

ELE-DS—

Next in family
