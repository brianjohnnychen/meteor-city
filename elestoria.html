Meteor City · Product line

Elestoria.
Hardware the data asked for.

Elestoria is the physical output of the Skyros pipeline — a line of computer and workspace accessories built where the numbers showed a gap. Laptop stands, USB hubs, and docking stations, sold at retail through Amazon, Walmart, and Target, and at wholesale to institutional buyers.

01 — Design process

From CAD file to production part.

Every Elestoria product moves through the same three states. It begins as parametric CAD, engineered against the specific shortcomings Skyros found in the incumbent products. It is then printed and lived with — prototypes go onto real desks until the geometry stops being noticed. Only then does it go to production tooling.

Phase 1 · CAD

Engineered to the data

Deck angle, depth, and thermal clearance are set by what the reviews of competing stands complained about — not by styling.

Phase 2 · Prototype

Printed, then lived with

Iterations run on real desks. A prototype passes when nobody thinks about it anymore — the geometry has disappeared into use.

Phase 3 · Production

Tooling and anodize

Final parts are CNC-formed and anodized aluminum, QC-inspected at the factory and again at the freight forwarder.
