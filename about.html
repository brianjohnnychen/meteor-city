01 — Story

How it started.

Meteor City was founded in July 2021 in Boston, Massachusetts by Brian Chen, a software engineer in the health-technology industry. Experience with clinical and operational data showed him the impact disciplined data can have — how it turns uncertain decisions into engineering problems.

He brought that discipline to e-commerce, where products can be built, launched, and sold without geographic limits. Skyros, the company’s internal data platform, informs what gets built; Elestoria is the product line that results — sold at retail through Amazon, Walmart, and Target, and at wholesale to organizations. Manufacturers also engage the company for market analysis and launches of their own.

02 — Operations

How it runs.

Products are manufactured with a set of vetted partner factories in Asia and inspected twice — at the factory and again at the freight forwarder — before anything ships. Fulfillment runs through Amazon’s network and third-party logistics partners across the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

Wholesale orders are handled separately from retail: configured, kitted, and delivered against the buyer’s procurement requirements. Where a requirement doesn’t match an existing product, the company designs and manufactures to specification.
